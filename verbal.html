A beginner's guide

verbal botheration

Call them names

The classic way in which one can bother their sibling is through calling them names. Feel free to get creative and personal - but, it's important to note, don't use slurs or hateful language in your name calling. Think of names like Turd Burger, King Fart, Booger Lord, and more. The grosser, the better (in our opinion at least)! Some more fun alternatives are squeaky greaseball, monster feet, mucus wagon, stool-boy, elbow, slime man jr., piss la croix, garbage legs, absolute loser, and Trump toupee.

Mimic the way they talk

The key to this one is not to poke fun of the way they talk so much as to repeat everyting back in as mocking a tone as possible. "I'm going to tell Mom" becomes a whiny "I'm gonna tell MooOOoom." A few examples demonstrating this can be heard in the audio clip below.

Deny Everything they say

That's terrible advice to follow. Don't do that. We didn't tell you to do that, did we? No. Of course not. Why on earth would we tell you that denying everything your sibling says would annoy them? Are you accusing us of saying that? We never did. How dare you?

Ask them why over and over

This is fairly self explanantory: reply to everything they say with "why?"

Sing obnoxious songs

Over the years, playground goers have come up with a host of the most annoying songs ever. These may vary regionally, but no doubt you've hear at least one of you elementary school classmates singing the same song over and over and over and over and over and over... at the top of their lungs. Employ this strategy when your sibling has stopped talking back to you and/or is ignoring you. Two example songs can be heard below.
